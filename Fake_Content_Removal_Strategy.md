## üîç GOOGLE CONTENT REMOVAL PROCESS

### A. Google Search Removal Options

#### 1. **Legal Removal Requests**
**URL**: https://support.google.com/websearch/answer/2744324

**Types of Removal**:
- **Copyright Infringement**: Use DMCA takedown
- **Trademark Violation**: For unauthorized use of company name/logo
- **Court Orders**: For legally proven defamation
- **Personal Information**: If sensitive data is exposed

**Process**:
```
Step 1: Go to https://support.google.com/legal/troubleshooter/1114905
Step 2: Select "Removal based on local law"
Step 3: Choose "India" (or your jurisdiction)
Step 4: Select appropriate category (defamation, trademark, etc.)
Step 5: Fill out detailed form with evidence
Step 6: Submit and wait for Google's review (2-4 weeks)
```

#### 2. **Google Ads Complaint**
If fake content appears in Google Ads:
- **URL**: https://support.google.com/google-ads/contact/vio_other_aw_policy
- Report trademark misuse in ads
- Faster removal (24-48 hours)

#### 3. **Google My Business**
For fake business listings:
- **URL**: https://support.google.com/business/answer/4669092
- Report duplicate or fake listings
- Claim your official listing first

#### 4. **YouTube (if applicable)**
- Use YouTube's copyright takedown: https://www.youtube.com/copyright_complaint_form
- Report impersonation: https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/2802027

### B. Direct Website Contact (Often Faster)

**Step-by-Step**:
1. Find website contact information (WHOIS lookup: https://who.is)
2. Send formal cease and desist letter
3. Request immediate content removal
4. Give 24-48 hour deadline
5. Follow up with legal action if ignored

## ‚öñÔ∏è LEGAL ACTION STEPS

### 1. Engage Legal Counsel Immediately

**Required Lawyers**:
- Intellectual Property (IP) lawyer
- Cyber law specialist
- Corporate litigation expert

### 2. Legal Options in India

#### A. **Criminal Complaints**
Under Indian Law:
- **IT Act 2000, Section 66D**: Punishment for cheating by impersonation using computer
- **IT Act 2000, Section 67**: Publishing false information in electronic form
- **Indian Penal Code 420**: Cheating and fraud
- **IPC 465-468**: Forgery and using forged documents
- **IPC 499-502**: Defamation

**Action**: File FIR at Cyber Crime Cell

#### B. **Civil Remedies**
- **Defamation Suit**: File civil case for damages
- **Injunction**: Court order to stop publishing
- **Trademark Infringement**: If logo/name misused

#### C. **SEBI Complaint**
Since you're a listed company:
- **URL**: https://scores.gov.in
- Report stock price manipulation
- SEBI takes action against market manipulation

### 3. Cease and Desist Letters

**Send to**:
- Website owners (via WHOIS contact)
- Blog platforms (Medium, Blogger, WordPress)
- Social media users (via platform)
- Hosting providers

**Template provided in next section**

### 4. Court Orders

**For persistent offenders**:
- File for restraining order
- Seek damages for business harm
- Request disclosure of anonymous users' identity

## üìù IMPORTANT REMINDERS

1. **Act Fast**: The longer fake content stays online, the more damage it causes
2. **Document Everything**: Screenshots, URLs, timestamps - evidence is crucial
3. **Be Consistent**: Use same messaging across all channels
4. **Stay Professional**: Never engage in arguments with fake accounts
5. **Legal First**: Always consult legal before public statements
6. **Protect Investors**: They are your priority in all communications
7. **Be Persistent**: Takedowns take time - don't give up
8. **Think Long-term**: Build systems to prevent future incidents
